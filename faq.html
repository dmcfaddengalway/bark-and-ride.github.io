Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you clip in. Can't find what you're looking for? Drop us a message.

Registration & Entry

You can register online through the Register button on our website. Registration is open now and places are limited, so we recommend signing up early to secure your spot on your preferred route.

Entry fees vary by route. All entry fees include route support, food stops, mechanical support, event jersey collection, and a finisher's medal. All proceeds go directly to Irish Guide Dogs for the Blind.

Yes, you can change your route up to one week before the event by contacting us via email. Route changes on the day are not possible for safety and logistics reasons.

Participants must be 16 or older for the 100km and 160km routes. The 50km route is open to riders aged 14 and above, provided they are accompanied by a registered adult.

On the Day

Registration and sign-in opens at 6:30am. The 160km route departs at 7:30am, the 100km route at 8:00am, and the 50km route at 8:30am. We recommend arriving at least 45 minutes before your start time.

The event starts and finishes in Barefield, Co. Clare. Full directions and parking information will be sent to all registered participants one week before the event.

Yes. All routes have fully stocked food and water stops at regular intervals. You can expect water, energy drinks, bananas, flapjacks, and other snacks. The 160km route has four stops, the 100km has three, and the 50km has two.

Yes, roaming mechanical support vehicles patrol all routes throughout the event. They can assist with punctures, minor repairs, and if necessary, transport you and your bike back to the finish.

The event runs rain or shine — this is Ireland after all! In the case of extreme weather conditions that pose a genuine safety risk, we may shorten or alter routes. Any changes will be communicated on the morning of the event.

Your Bike & Gear

A road bike is recommended for all routes. Hybrid or gravel bikes are fine for the 50km route. E-bikes are welcome on all routes. Mountain bikes and bikes with bar-end extensions are not permitted for safety reasons.

Yes, absolutely. A properly fitted, CE-certified cycling helmet is mandatory for all participants on all routes. You will not be permitted to start without one — no exceptions.

We recommend: your bike (in good working order), helmet, spare inner tubes, tyre levers, a pump or CO2 inflator, a rain jacket, filled water bottles, and your phone with an emergency contact saved. Your event number must be visible on your bike at all times.

Fundraising & Charity

There is no mandatory fundraising minimum — your entry fee alone makes a meaningful contribution to Irish Guide Dogs. However, if you'd like to raise additional funds, we provide every participant with a personal fundraising page and tips to help you reach your goal.

All proceeds go directly to Irish Guide Dogs for the Blind. This funds the training and placement of guide dogs for people with vision impairment, and assistance dogs for families of children with autism. It costs approximately €50,000 to train and place a single guide dog.

Yes! When you register, you can create or join a team. Each team gets a shared fundraising page alongside individual pages. It's a great way to motivate each other and track your collective impact.

Still have questions? Get in touch at barkandride@guidedogs.ie or find us on social media.
