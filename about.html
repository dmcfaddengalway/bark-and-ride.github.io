About Us

A charity cycle in aid of Irish Guide Dogs — bringing riders together to change lives, one kilometre at a time.

The Cycle. The Cause. The Community.

Bark & Ride is a charity cycling sportive held in Galway, Ireland, in support of Irish Guide Dogs. Whether you're a seasoned cyclist or lacing up your cleats for the first time, this event brings people together for a day of incredible riding — and an even greater cause.

With three routes to choose from (60km, 90km, and 125km), riders of all levels are welcome. Every entry fee, every fundraised euro, and every kilometre pedalled goes directly to supporting the training and placement of guide dogs and assistance dogs across Ireland.

Our Charity Partner

One Community, Changing Lives

Irish Guide Dogs is Ireland's national charity dedicated to enabling people who are vision impaired and families of children with autism to achieve improved mobility and independence.

For over 50 years, they have worked with a community of breeders, puppy raisers, home socialisers, trainers, staff, volunteers and supporters to change as many lives as possible. All of their services are provided completely free of charge.

Guide Dogs

Highly trained dogs that provide safe, independent mobility for people with vision impairment — giving freedom, confidence and companionship.

Assistance Dogs

Specially trained dogs placed with families of children with autism, providing safety, calming support and helping the whole family engage with their community.

Community Dogs

Visiting teams that bring the therapeutic benefits of dogs to hospitals, care homes, schools and community groups throughout Ireland.
